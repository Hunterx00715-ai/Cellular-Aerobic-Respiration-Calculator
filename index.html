Electron Transport System (ETS)

ETS is the last step of aerobic respiration which occurs in the inner membrane of "Mitochondria". There are 5 complexes which help in the process of ETS:

  1. Complex-1: NADH+H+ dehydrogenase
  2. Complex-2: FADH2 dehydrogenase
  3. Complex-3: Cytochrome b-c1
  4. Complex-4: Cytochrome a-a3 or Cytochrome c-oxidase
  5. Complex-5: ATP synthetase

In ETS, Complex-1 and Complex-2 work independently from each other.

Process goes for "Complex-1" is:
NADH+H+ breakdown into NAD+ + 2H+ because of NADH+H+ dehydrogenase. Now, the two protons (H+) taken by the mobile carrier "Ubiquinone" placed in the inner side inner membrane of "Mitochondria" (converts into "Ubiquinol") as hydrogens, then, it passes the electrons to "Complex-3" by removing the protons in the matrix of "Mitochondria", now, the electrons go from "Complex-3" to another mobile carrier, a protein one, "Cytochrome-c" placed on the outer side of inner membrane of "Mitochondria", it passes the electrons further to "Complex-4".

Within this process of "Complex-1" 4 protons get pumped out of "Complex-1", 4 protons from "Complex-3" and 2 protons from "Complex-4", in the inter membrane of "Mitochondria" and remaining 2 protons taken by the oxygen to get converted into water. Basically, total 10 protons get pumped by NADH+H+ in ETS.

Process goes for "Complex-2" is:
FADH2 breakdown into FAD+ + 2H+ because of NADH+H+ dehydrogenase. Now, the two protons (H+) taken by the mobile carrier "Ubiquinone" placed in the inner side inner membrane of "Mitochondria" (converts into "Ubiquinol") as hydrogens, then, it passes the electrons to "Complex-3" by removing the protons in the matrix of "Mitochondria", now, the electrons go from "Complex-3" to another mobile carrier, a protein one, "Cytochrome-c" placed on the outer side of inner membrane of "Mitochondria", it passes the electrons further to "Complex-4".

Within this process of "Complex-2" 4 protons from "Complex-3" and 2 protons from "Complex-4", in the inter membrane of "Mitochondria" and remaining 2 protons taken by the oxygen to get converted into water. Basically, total 6 protons get pumped by FADH2 in ETS.

ATP Formation:
4 protons needed to form one ATP through "Complex-5".
For NADH+H+, 10 protons will get converted into 2.5 ATP.
For FADH2, 6 protons will get converted into 1.5 ATP.
